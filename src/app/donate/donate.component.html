Help Us Save the Earth!

 

Founded in 1972, the current objectives of the Save The Earth Foundation are to provide factual educational information to the public about environmental awareness, while helping to fund a variety of environmental initiatives as a non-profit public benefit corporation. We currently aim to fund the single most impacting environmental changes worldwide. The overall objective of the Save the Earth Foundation is to enhance the quality of our global environment for the benefit of all people and promoting a better understanding of the effects our society has on the long-term health of our planet.

The Save the Earth Foundation is committed to raising public environmental consciousness, as well as actively implementing change. Previously, the Save the Earth Foundation funding has helped to support scientific environmental research and educational programs in top institutions of higher education. Our generous donations made it possible to fund the Climate Center at Columbia University, which analyses and evaluates the current state of our atmosphere. Similarly, our donations have helped to build UCLA’s Center for the Study of the Environment and Society, which focuses on the interactions between the environmental and social processes. We are proud to have also funded the Phytotron Project at Duke University, which focuses on the consequences of global warming through large-scale modeling.

Thanks to these climate centers and programs, we currently have much more access to environmental scientific discoveries. Presently, the foundation commits to funding the most impacting global environmental initiatives in order to actively create the change.

With the support of people worldwide from all walks of life, we are confident that the Foundation’s efforts will help to reverse the environmental deterioration of our planet.

Thank you for your concern with regards our planet’s future.
